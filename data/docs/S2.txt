TITLE: Clinical trial protocol structure (mock)
YEAR: 2022
TEXT:
A protocol often includes Background, Objectives, Study Design, Eligibility, Endpoints, and Safety monitoring.
Methodology should specify randomization, blinding, and statistical considerations at a high level.
Clear definition of primary and secondary endpoints is critical for regulatory submission and approval.
Inclusion and exclusion criteria must be unambiguously detailed to ensure a homogeneous study population.
The schedule of assessments dictates the frequency of clinic visits, blood draws, and imaging procedures.
Data management plans outline how clinical data will be collected, validated, and securely stored.
Ethical considerations, including informed consent procedures and Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval, are mandatory.
Adverse event reporting timelines are strictly governed by Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines.
Statistical analysis plans (SAP) are finalized prior to database lock to prevent bias.
Protocol amendments must be carefully documented and approved by all relevant ethical committees.
