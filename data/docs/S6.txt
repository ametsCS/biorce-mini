TITLE: Patient-Reported Outcomes in Clinical Research (mock)
YEAR: 2022
TEXT:
Patient-Reported Outcomes (PROs) provide direct assessments of a patient's health status without interpretation by a clinician.
In respiratory trials, validated instruments such as the Asthma Control Questionnaire (ACQ) are widely utilized.
Electronic PRO (ePRO) systems have significantly improved data collection compliance compared to traditional paper diaries.
Regulatory agencies like the FDA emphasize PROs to demonstrate meaningful clinical benefit beyond physiological measurements.
Translation and cultural adaptation of PRO instruments are essential for global, multi-center clinical trials.
Recall bias is a common limitation, which is why daily ePRO entries are often preferred over weekly or monthly questionnaires.
Missing data handling in PRO analysis requires sophisticated statistical methods to avoid biased conclusions.
The minimally important difference (MID) defines the smallest change in a PRO score that is clinically meaningful to the patient.
Integrating PRO data with wearable device data represents the next frontier in continuous patient monitoring during trials.
Successful implementation of PROs requires early planning and selection of fit-for-purpose measurement instruments.
