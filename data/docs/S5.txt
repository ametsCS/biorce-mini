TITLE: Good Clinical Practice and Data Integrity (mock)
YEAR: 2020
TEXT:
Adherence to Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines is mandatory for the ethical and scientific integrity of clinical trials.
Key principles include obtaining informed consent, ensuring the safety of trial subjects, and maintaining verifiable records.
Investigators are responsible for the medical care of trial subjects and must report serious adverse events (SAEs) promptly.
Sponsors must implement robust quality assurance and quality control systems throughout the trial lifecycle.
Regular monitoring visits are conducted to verify that study procedures comply with the approved protocol.
Data integrity is paramount, requiring secure electronic data capture (EDC) systems with comprehensive audit trails.
Source document verification (SDV) ensures that the data entered into the clinical database matches the original medical records.
Any deviations from the clinical protocol must be documented, explained, and reported to the sponsor and IRB.
Archiving of essential documents must comply with regulatory retention periods after the completion of the trial.
Continuous training of site staff is required to maintain high standards of clinical data collection and patient safety.
