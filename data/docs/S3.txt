TITLE: Biomarkers in Atopic Dermatitis trials (mock)
YEAR: 2023
TEXT:
Atopic dermatitis is characterized by Type 2 inflammation, with cytokines like IL-4 and IL-13 playing central roles.
Biomarker analysis in clinical trials often involves measuring serum IgE levels and specific chemokines like TARC (CCL17).
Stratification of patients based on baseline biomarker profiles helps identify likely responders to targeted therapies.
Skin biopsy transcriptomics provide deep insights into the mechanism of action of investigational dermatological drugs.
The correlation between systemic biomarkers in blood and localized skin severity scores (like EASI) is actively investigated.
Recent trials emphasize the need for non-invasive biomarker collection, such as tape stripping techniques.
Monitoring changes in biomarker levels during treatment can serve as an early indicator of therapeutic efficacy.
Predictive biomarkers are highly sought after to personalize treatment and minimize unnecessary immunosuppressant exposure.
Standardization of assay methodologies across different clinical trial sites remains a logistical challenge.
Future trials will likely incorporate multi-omics approaches to fully characterize the patient's baseline inflammatory state.
