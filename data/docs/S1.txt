TITLE: Asthma biologics overview (mock)
YEAR: 2023
TEXT:
Biologic therapies targeting IL-5 are used in severe eosinophilic asthma to reduce exacerbations.
Trials typically define inclusion criteria based on exacerbation history and biomarkers.
Safety monitoring commonly includes adverse event tracking and periodic clinical assessments.
In severe phenotypes, the reduction of oral corticosteroid dependence is a primary endpoint.
Monoclonal antibodies have reshaped the treatment landscape, offering targeted intervention.
Patient selection heavily relies on baseline blood eosinophil counts and fractional exhaled nitric oxide (FeNO).
Real-world evidence largely supports the efficacy observed in randomized controlled trials.
Long-term extension studies confirm the sustained safety profile of these interventions over multiple years.
The cost-effectiveness of these treatments remains a topic of ongoing health economics research.
Future directions include exploring the efficacy of combining different biologic agents.
